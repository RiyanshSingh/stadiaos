# StadiaOS Demo Script

To properly evaluate StadiaOS, you need to see how the Fan App and Ops Command Center interact in real time. Follow this script to experience the full end-to-end flow.

## Initial Setup
Ensure you have run `schema.sql` and `seed.sql` in Supabase, and have the local dev server running (`npm run dev`).

Open two browser windows side-by-side:
- **Window 1 (Fan App):** Navigate to `http://localhost:5173/`
- **Window 2 (Ops Command Center):** Navigate to `http://localhost:5173/ops`

---

## 1. Fan App Basics & Map
In the **Fan App (Window 1)**:
1. Notice the live dashboard. It pulls real facility recommendations and alerts from the database.
2. Click **Map** in the bottom navigation.
3. Observe the rendered stadium graph. The nodes (facilities, gates) are hydrated from the seeded database.

## 2. Copilot & Live Routing
In the **Fan App (Window 1)**, click the glowing AI Assistant FAB (bottom right).

1. **Ask:** *"Where is the nearest washroom?"*
   - Copilot will extract the `FACILITY_LOOKUP` intent, query the real facility services, and return an interactive card showing the nearest washroom with its live wait time.
2. **Ask:** *"Take me to my seat"*
   - Copilot extracts the `ROUTE_HANDOFF` intent. It calculates the ETA based on the static routing graph from your current location (defaulted to your ticket's gate) to your section.
3. **Click "Open in Map"** on the generated route card.
   - You will be deep-linked into the Route Detail view.
   - Observe the step-by-step walking directions generated by the custom Dijkstra routing engine.
   - Click the **Accessible Route** toggle to see the engine recalculate a path that avoids stairs.

## 3. Incident Reporting (Fan to Ops)
Let's simulate an emergency.

1. In the **Fan App**, open the Copilot again.
2. **Type:** *"Someone fainted near section 214"*
   - Copilot extracts an `INCIDENT_DRAFT` intent, classifying it as a **Medical** issue with **High** severity, and prepares a draft card.
3. Click **Submit Report**.
4. Immediately look at the **Ops Command Center (Window 2)**.
   - In the **Incident Triage Desk**, you will see the new Medical incident pop up instantly via Supabase Realtime.
   - Note the "AI Analysis" section inside the incident card—our AI has automatically generated a brief operational summary for staff.

## 4. Triage Workflow (Ops)
In the **Ops Command Center (Window 2)**:
1. Click on the newly reported medical incident to expand it.
2. Click **Medical** under Assign Team to dispatch a medical unit.
3. Click **In Progress** to update the status.
4. Type a quick note in the "Add Note" field (e.g., "Unit 4 dispatched") and hit enter. 

*Behind the scenes, these actions are logged in the `incident_updates` table to maintain a full audit trail.*

## 5. Broadcasting Advisories (Ops to Fan)
Let's inform the rest of the fans about a delay.

1. In the **Ops Command Center**, look at the **Public Advisory** composer (below triage).
2. Enter the Headline: *"Medical Emergency near Section 214"*
3. Enter the Message: *"Please avoid the south concourse while medical teams respond to an incident."*
4. Click **Broadcast Advisory**.
5. Immediately look at the **Fan App (Window 1)**.
   - Click on **Alerts** in the bottom navigation (or go to the Home dashboard).
   - You will see the new advisory instantly appear at the top of the feed, demonstrating real-time ops-to-fan communication.
